THE FLYING DEZIRE BAND

We love music!

We are Flying Dezire, an experimental rockband from the Raipur, Chhattisgarh, India with a 100% self-written repertoire.

The Flying Dezire began to take shape when two music lovers and long lost friends, Aman Sen and Santosh Sethi met in Raipur and decided to live their passion of creating music. They chanced upon the band prodigy Aayangshu Chatterjee, a local boy, Jay Chauhan as bassist and a music enthusiast Ekash Dewangan, from Raipur itself who then completed The Flying Dezire. From its very inception, the band began its unceasing journey of several milestones of success beginning with the band event at Avirbhaav, the headlining Annual event of National Institute of Technology, Raipur. Mithilesh Ghrithlahere later joined the band as a keyboard player to provide a scintillating backup to the band with his talent. To provide more versatility in the rhythm section.

OUTLINE:

The Flying Dezire revolves around Aman's grunge and clean vocals providing a powerful combination of passion and compassion. Santosh handle the beats, over which Aayangshu & Ekansh play a combination of riffs & solos, adding melody and soul to the art. Jay backs up the rhythm by handling the bass section while Mithilesh adds to the flavor of the songs with the piano and the keys. The Flying Dezire is a Hindi Band, that claims to be nothing else but original and deep seated in its roots of Indian Music.
We are releasing our debut album soon, stay tuned for more updates.

Mithilesh Ghritlahere,    Keyboardist


“I had never before thought of how awful the relationship must be between the musician and his instrument. He has to fill it, this instrument, with the breath of life, his own. He has to make it do what he wants it to do. And a piano is just a piano. It’s made out of so much wood and wires and little hammers and big ones, and ivory. While there’s only so much you can do with it, the only way to find this out is to try; to try and make it do everything.”

Aman Sen,    Lead Vocalist


“Writing, painting, singing- it cannot stop everything. Cannot halt death in its tracks. But perhaps it can make the pause between death’s footsteps sound and look and feel beautiful, can make the space of waiting a place where you can linger without as much fear. For we are all walking each other to our deaths, and the journey there between footsteps makes up our lives.”

Santosh Sethi,    Drummer


When you're writing, you're only a brain and some fingers, but drumming, you're involving all four limbs, and you're hearing stuff and you're converting your ideas into physical motions, getting physical feedback from things you are touching - it's pretty cool. It's a really a nice contrast to writing.

Ekansh Dewangan,    Lead Guitarist


"When you're first starting out, there's always the temptation to hide behind distortion because it lets you get away with murder. But, when it comes to rhythm work, you've gotta back off that gain control a bit, especially if you're playing with another guitarist."

Ayanangshu Chatterjee,    Rhythm Guitarist


"Every guitarist I would cross paths with would tell me that I should have a flashy guitar, whatever the latest fashion model was, and I used to say, 'Why? Mine works, doesn't it? It's a piece of wood and six strings, and it works."

Jay Chauhan,    Bassist


"I play bass. I play a bit of guitar. I've never been to a lesson, so my theory of music is non-existent in any instrument, but we always had guitars around. My dad taught me to play drums for 'Love Actually,' and I still play drums now. But I'm not a 'drummer.' I'm not a 'guitarist.' I'm trying to be a bassist"
